## **Connecting to Last Week**

Today we will continue where we left off in our reading last week.  The previous sermon was on Philippians 1:12-14\.  Those verses revealed to us how a sovereign God can use our circumstances for His glory.  Not only for His glory, but for His mission as well.   No matter the evil intentions of man, satan, or our own flesh, God's plans _will_ succeed.

We saw this in the way Paul viewed his imprisonment.  Those who imprisoned Paul sought to halt the spread of the Gospel.  They thought that putting Paul behind bars would keep him quiet, or at least keep his message off the streets.  If the message belonged only to Paul, then this strategy might have worked.  We can find stories of people throughout history who had radical ideas.  A few months, years, or decades in prison was an effective strategy for battling the spread of those ideas.

Yet, Paul's message did not actually belong to Paul.  Paul's message was the good news of God's Kingdom, fulfilled in the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus.  We call that message the Gospel, and the Gospel message - from beginning to end - belonged to God, and it belongs to Him today!  God is the owner of the message, and the advancement of that message is an essential part of His plan to redeem this world.

Thus, the imprisonment of Paul could not stop the spread of the Gospel.  In fact, in that classic God way, it worked to the very opposite of what men and Satan intended for it.
