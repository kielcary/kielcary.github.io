I am a pastor living in McCook, Nebraska.  Melissa and I were married in 2010, and we have three boys (Jonas, Samuel, and Benjamin). 

I grew up in Chadron, Nebraska, where I graduated from high school and then from Chadron State College in 2005.  I spent several years working as a software developer, and then joined the United States Army in 2018 where I served in the 82nd Airborne Division.  At the end of that grand adventure, we moved to Cincinnati, Ohio, where enrolled in seminary at Cincinnati Christian University.  We moved back to McCook in 2016, where I transferred to Regent University to complete my Master’s of Divinity online.

We began attending Grace Baptist Church in 2017 after I completed an internship in student ministry with McCook Christian Church. I assumed part-time pastoral duties in early 2019 from my friend and mentor, Brett Scollard.  I became the full-time pastor in July of that year after the church re-planted as [Grace Fellowship](http://gracemccook.com) in downtown McCook.

I love studying theology, hearing the inspirational stories of redemption from other Christians, reading, writing, music, hiking, camping - the list goes on.  Creation is a wonderful thing to explore.  Thankfully, I have the best partner in exploration I could ask for in the form of my wife, and the three most incredible little men to show creation to in the form of my boys.
