Hello! My name is Kiel Cary, and I am a full stack .NET developer living in McCook, Nebraska. I married Melissa in July 2010, and we have three boys (Jonas, Samuel, and Benjamin).

I grew up in Chadron, Nebraska, where I graduated from high school and then from Chadron State College in 2005. I spent several years working as a software and web application developer for Cabela’s, Inc., and then joined the United States Army in 2010 where I served in the 82nd Airborne Division. At the end of that grand adventure, we moved to Cincinnati, Ohio, where I enrolled in seminary at Cincinnati Christian University. I worked full time as a software developer for SeamlessEHR developing primarily in C#, WPF, MSSQL, and related .NET technologies.

Our family moved to McCook, Nebraska, in 2016. I transferred to Regent University to complete my Master’s of Divinity online while working for American Agricultural Laboratory developing C#/Winforms desktop applications and ASP.NET MVC web applications. I was given the privilege to step in to lead our small church as pastor in early 2019. After initially starting off part-time as pastor, I became the full-time pastor in May 2019 and resigned from the laboratory. I graduated from Regent University with my M.Div. in December 2019. 

I handed the church off to a young, promising pastor in June 2020. I had been working part-time as a .NET consultant during my time as a pastor and have returned to development full time.

I love reading, writing, studying theology, music, hiking, camping, grilling - the list goes on. Creation is a wonderful thing to explore. Thankfully, I have the best partner in exploration I could ask for in the form of my wife, and the three most incredible little men to show creation to in the form of my boys.
